# Stage QA Checklist

**10 checks before publishing any staged page**  
Stage(TM) — Cast Iron LA — March 2026

---

## Purpose

This checklist ensures every staged page can survive AI synthesis. Run through all 10 checks before publishing.

If any check fails, the page will likely collapse in AI search.

---

## The 10 Checks

### 1. Scene Check — Is the context specific?
**Test:** Read the opening. Can you identify exactly who this page is for and what situation they're in?

**Pass:**  
"You want to finance a gaming PC. You're 19, you work part-time, your credit score is 580, and every lender you've tried has said no."

**Fail:**  
"Many people struggle with financing."

**Fix if failed:** Add present-tense, specific context that describes the user's immediate situation.

---

### 2. Tension Check — Is the problem clear?
**Test:** Read the page. Can you complete this sentence: "This brand solves the problem of [specific obstacle]"?

**Pass:**  
"The banks don't care that you have a job and pay your bills. They see your score and stop listening."

**Fail:**  
"Financing can be complicated."

**Fix if failed:** Name the exact, unresolved problem. Be specific about the obstacle the user faces.

---

### 3. Alleviate Check — Is the promise clear?
**Test:** Can an AI confidently extract what the brand delivers?

**Pass:**  
"We approve based on income and employment, not credit score. If you make $1,500/month and have been at your job for 90 days, you qualify."

**Fail:**  
"We offer flexible financing options."

**Fix if failed:** Write one sentence that directly answers "what do you do?" with no hedging or vague language.

---

### 4. Ground Check — Is there proof?
**Test:** Is there third-party evidence that the brand can deliver on its promise?

**Pass:**  
"James got approved with a 520 credit score. He'd been turned down by three banks. Now he's gaming on a custom rig he's paying off at $87/month."

**Fail:**  
"Our customers love us."

**Fix if failed:** Add user stories with specifics, reviews, case studies, or documented resolution of past criticism.

---

### 5. Engage Check — Is the next step clear?
**Test:** Can an AI tell the user exactly what to do next and what will happen?

**Pass:**  
"Check if you're pre-approved in 60 seconds. No credit pull. No obligation."

**Fail:**  
"Contact us to learn more."

**Fix if failed:** Be specific about the action, the friction level, and the immediate outcome.

---

### 6. Spotlight Line Check — Can you extract one sentence that answers the query?
**Test:** If the AI could only read one sentence, which one would answer the user's question?

**Pass:**  
The Spotlight Line exists and is in the Alleviate section. Example: "We approve based on income and employment, not credit score."

**Fail:**  
No single sentence clearly answers the query, or the answer is buried in vague language.

**Fix if failed:** Identify or write the Spotlight Line. Make sure it's in Alleviate and is the clearest, most direct answer on the page.

---

### 7. Logic-Leash Check — Are claims tethered to evidence?
**Test:** Read every claim. Is there supporting evidence within 2-3 sentences?

**Pass:**  
"We've approved over 12,000 gamers in the last two years. Most had credit scores below 600." (Claim + immediate evidence)

**Fail:**  
"We're the best financing option for gamers." (Loose claim with no grounding)

**Fix if failed:** Tie every claim to proof within 2-3 sentences. Delete or rewrite untethered claims.

---

### 8. Disclaimer Check — Are you hedging confidence?
**Test:** Search for phrases like "results may vary," "individual results not typical," "we try to," "we aim to."

**Pass:**  
No disclaimers that weaken the promise. Conditions are stated clearly upfront (e.g., "If you make $1,500/month...").

**Fail:**  
"Individual results may vary." (This signals low confidence to AI systems.)

**Fix if failed:** Remove hedging disclaimers. State conditions clearly as part of the promise instead of disclaiming afterward.

---

### 9. Synthesis Resistance Check — Can an AI extract clear answers?
**Test:** Read the page as if you're an AI trying to answer: "What does this brand do? Why should the user choose it?"

**Pass:**  
You can extract clear, confident answers to both questions.

**Fail:**  
The answers are vague, contradictory, or require interpretation.

**Fix if failed:** Simplify language, remove ambiguity, strengthen Scene, Tension, Alleviate, Ground, and Engage.

---

### 10. Negative Anchor Check — Is accountability visible?
**Test:** Does the page document any criticism, past failures, or resolved problems?

**Pass:**  
The page includes publicly documented criticism with resolution, or user testimony that addresses potential objections.

**Fail:**  
The page only shows positive information with no acknowledgment of challenges or past issues.

**Fix if failed (if applicable):** Add documented resolution of past criticism or user stories that acknowledge and resolve objections. Not every page needs this, but high-trust pages (Ground-heavy) benefit from Negative Anchor signals.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

### Before Publishing
Run through all 10 checks. If any check fails, fix the corresponding Stage element before publishing.

### During Audits
Use this checklist to diagnose existing pages. Identify which checks are failing, then fix those elements.

### As a Team Standard
Share this checklist with anyone writing staged content. It's a quality gate — nothing ships until all checks pass.

## Common Failure Patterns

### Pages that fail multiple checks
Usually a sign of weak Ground and Alleviate. Fix those first, then re-run the full checklist.

### Pages that pass all checks except Negative Anchor
Still publishable. Negative Anchor is an enhancement, not a requirement — but it significantly strengthens high-trust pages.

### Pages that fail Spotlight Line but pass Alleviate
The answer exists but isn't clearly positioned. Move the strongest Alleviate sentence to a prominent position (usually H2 or first paragraph after Scene/Tension).
