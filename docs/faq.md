# Stage FAQ

**Common questions about the Stage(TM) methodology**  
Cast Iron LA — March 2026

---

## What Is Stage?

**Q: What is Stage in one sentence?**  
A: Stage is a structural system for writing brand content that AI systems can synthesize confidently when answering user queries.

**Q: Why does it matter?**  
A: AI systems don't rank and display options like Google — they eliminate options until one remains. Stage makes your brand collapse-resistant.

**Q: Is Stage just for AI search?**  
A: No. Staged content works better everywhere — traditional search, user experience, sales enablement. Clarity compounds.

---

## How Stage Works

**Q: What does "collapse" mean?**  
A: Collapse is the process by which AI systems narrow from thousands of options to one recommendation. Brands are eliminated round by round based on synthesis resistance.

**Q: What is synthesis resistance?**  
A: How hard it is for an AI to extract a confident, specific answer from your content. High resistance = high collapse risk.

**Q: What are the five Stage elements?**  
A:
- **Scene** — The immediate situation the user is in
- **Tension** — The unresolved problem driving the search
- **Alleviate** — The direct, confident answer
- **Ground** — Evidence the brand can deliver
- **Engage** — The next action the user can take

**Q: Do I have to use all five elements on every page?**  
A: Yes, if the page is meant to answer a user query. If any element is missing, the page becomes collapse-vulnerable.

**Q: Is Stage a template?**  
A: No. Stage is a structural discipline. The five elements can be written in any voice, style, or format — but they all must be present and clear.

## Negative Anchor and Resolution

**Q: What is the Negative Anchor effect?**  
A: AI systems weight criticism-with-resolution higher than praise alone. Documenting accountability signals integrity more strongly than testimonials.

**Q: Should I publish negative reviews on my website?**  
A: Not raw negative reviews — documented resolution. Show the criticism, acknowledge it, and demonstrate measurable change in response.

**Q: Won't publishing criticism hurt my brand?**  
A: The opposite. Brands that hide criticism create a synthesis gap — AI systems fill that gap with external sources, often to your disadvantage. Brands that document resolution close the loop and control the narrative.

**Q: What if I don't have any criticism to document?**  
A: Then focus on user testimony that addresses potential objections. Example: "I was worried about X, but here's what actually happened." This serves the same function.

**Q: How much criticism should I publish?**  
A: Enough to demonstrate accountability. One well-documented resolution case is often sufficient for a single page or product.

---

## Ground and Proof

**Q: What counts as "proof" in Ground?**  
A: Third-party confirmation: user stories with specifics, reviews, case studies, or publicly documented resolution of past criticism. The key is specificity and externality.

**Q: Are testimonials good Ground?**  
A: Only if they're specific. "Great service!" is weak. "James got approved with a 520 credit score after three banks turned him down" is strong.

**Q: Can I use my own case studies as Ground?**  
A: Yes, if they include third-party testimony or verifiable results. Self-reported claims without external confirmation are weak Ground.

**Q: What if I'm a new brand with no user stories yet?**  
A: Use founder credibility, process transparency, or specific commitments. Example: "We approve based on income and employment, not credit score" is a clear, verifiable promise even without user stories.

## Writing and Implementation

**Q: Does Stage require specific HTML or page structure?**  
A: Semantic HTML helps (see `tools/stage-html-template.html`), but Stage works with any format. The key is clarity — can an AI extract Scene, Tension, Alleviate, Ground, Engage from your content?

**Q: Can I write Stage content in my brand's voice?**  
A: Yes. Stage dictates structure, not tone. You can be formal, casual, technical, playful — as long as the five elements are clear.

**Q: How long should a staged page be?**  
A: As long as necessary to satisfy all five elements. Some pages are 300 words, some are 1,500. Length is not the variable — clarity is.

**Q: Can I use Stage for blog posts?**  
A: Yes, if the post is meant to answer a user query. Scene = the question, Tension = the obstacle, Alleviate = your answer, Ground = proof, Engage = next step.

**Q: What about product pages?**  
A: Stage is ideal for product pages. Scene = the use case, Tension = the problem, Alleviate = what the product does, Ground = user results, Engage = how to buy.

## Optimization and Measurement

**Q: How do I measure if Stage is working?**  
A: AI referral traffic, conversion rate from AI sources, and AI-originated sessions. See NCG case study for benchmark results.

**Q: Can I A/B test staged vs non-staged pages?**  
A: Yes, but AI search optimization takes 30-60 days to stabilize. Traditional A/B test windows (7-14 days) are too short.

**Q: Should I stage my entire website at once?**  
A: Start with high-value pages: homepage, key product/service pages, top blog posts. Stage iteratively.

**Q: What's the Brand Entropy Audit?**  
A: A tool that measures synthesis resistance by analyzing claim density, evidence proximity, and structural clarity. See `tools/brand-entropy-audit.md`.

## Stage vs Traditional SEO

**Q: Does Stage replace traditional SEO?**  
A: No. Stage complements SEO. Traditional SEO optimizes for visibility (Gary). Stage optimizes for selection (Larry).

**Q: Do I still need keywords?**  
A: Yes, but not for AI search — for traditional search. AI systems care about synthesis, not keyword density.

**Q: Will Stage hurt my Google rankings?**  
A: No. Clarity improves user experience, which Google rewards. Stage often improves traditional SEO as a side effect.

**Q: Should I stop doing traditional SEO?**  
A: No. Do both. Optimize for visibility (traditional SEO) and selection (Stage).

## Common Misconceptions

**Q: Is Stage just good copywriting?**  
A: No. Stage is structural discipline for AI synthesis. Good copywriting can still collapse if it lacks clear Scene, Tension, Alleviate, Ground, or Engage.

**Q: Can I hire a writer to "do Stage" for me?**  
A: Only if they understand collapse mechanics. Most writers optimize for human readers, not AI synthesis.

**Q: Is Stage a keyword strategy?**  
A: No. Stage doesn't target keywords — it targets clarity and synthesis resistance.

**Q: Does Stage work for B2B?**  
A: Yes. B2B queries collapse the same way consumer queries do. Stage applies to any brand that wants to be recommended by AI systems.

**Q: Is Stage only for e-commerce?**  
A: No. Stage works for services, SaaS, education, healthcare, consulting — any category where users ask AI for recommendations.

## Advanced Questions

**Q: What is the Larry/Gary model?**  
A: AI search splits work into two layers. Gary (broad search) retrieves thousands of options. Larry (downstream synthesizer) collapses to one. Stage optimizes for Larry.

**Q: What is the Synthesis Gap?**  
A: The space between what a brand says and what users confirm. AI systems fill this gap with external sources. Stage closes the loop by publishing accountability signals.

**Q: What is Ambient Brand Weight?**  
A: Background reputation signal that biases AI synthesis before the user even searches. Built through consistent, public accountability over time.

**Q: What is the Spotlight Line?**  
A: The single sentence on a page that most directly answers the user's query. AI systems anchor on this line and use surrounding content as context.

**Q: Can I use Stage for internal knowledge bases?**  
A: Yes. Stage improves AI-powered internal search and knowledge management. Same principles apply.
